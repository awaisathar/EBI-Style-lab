Our logo and name

The name of our institute in printed publications is "European Molecular Biology Laboratory - European Bioinformatics Institute (EMBL-EBI)".

In our logo, without exception, the name "EMBL" or "EMBL-EBI" is placed to the left of and in a defined ratio to the EMBL spot icon.

There should always be a blank space, or 'protected area' around the EMBL-EBI logo that sets it apart from margins and any ensuing text or images.

This protected area corresponds to one single length of the spot logo.

The EMBL-EBI logo may only be set against a white, black or EMBL-blue background.

Against a coloured background, "EMBL-EBI" should appear in white typeface. Against a clear background, it should appear in black typeface.

A greyscale version of the logo is also available.

EMBL fonts

EMBL uses Helvetica Neue Pro and Minion Pro fonts for print. EMBL-EBI's website also uses Verdana.

If you cannot use Helvetica, Arial may be used.

For those keenly interested in fonts: Originally designed in 1959 by Max Miedinger, the Helvetica font was adapted by the D. Stempel AG company and put on the market in 1983 under the name of New Helvetica. It is available as a complete font family with 51 types and weights. Minion was originally designed as a traditional typeset font by American Robert Slimbach in 1992.

For continuous text we recommend 10-point Minion with a 12-point leading. (Leadings in MS Word are shown in the box underneath font size.)

## Slide sets and slide template

Guidelines for use

EMBL is a well-known institute with an international reputation, and it is important that we ensure it is clearly recognisable to the outside world. All slide presentations, posters, business cards, print publications, web pages, letters and faxes should use specific colours and fonts so that we present a uniform design.

This slide template and sample presentations are provided for internal and external use, and are updated periodically. If you require these materials in a different format, please send a request to External Relations.

The EMBL-EBI slide template

The Slide Template is created and maintained by Spencer Phillips in External Relations.

To use the template:

  • Make sure you have the EMBL-EBI fonts installed on your machine
  • Download the file
  • "Save As" a PowerPoint template
  • Close the file, then select "New from template"
  • Open the file from "My templates".

You will be able to create new slides and choose from a selection of backgrounds (use the "Layout" commands).

A Keynote slide template is also available.

Tips for making a good poster

  • Put yourself in the viewer's shoes.
    - What are they MOST interested in?
    - How close will they get to read the poster?

  • Divide your content up into 'chunks', or paragraphs.
    - Decide which chunks are crucial, necessary and nice to have.

  • The 'crucial' chunks should be the most prominent.
    - The biggest of these should be your 'elevator pitch' - the main thing you found.

  • Consider carefully how much content you need to generate interest.
    - Do you need those 'nice to haves'?
    - How much of your methodology do you need to show?

  • If you can't read the text from a metre away, it is not likely to be read by many people.

  • What is the central image in your poster, and why?
    - Do you need more imagery?
    - Are your graphs helping tell a story?

  • If you need guidance, or would like a second opinion about your poster, bring it along to External Relations in A2-120 (East Wing, ground floor).
    - You can also email it to comms@ebi.ac.uk
